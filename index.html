Team Structure

The Event will work on Four Groups of Engineering Stream,viz:

  • Civil + Planning
  • Mechnical + Production
  • Computer + Information Technology
  • Electrical + Electronics & Telecommunication + Instrumentation

The Participating Colleges can send their teams in following ways:

A. Team of Five (2 + 2 + 1) :
Two students each from any two out of the four groups mentioned above. The Fifth member will strengthen any one of the two groups you send.
B. Team of Seven (2 + 2 + 2 + 1) :
Two students each from any three out of the four groups mentioned above. The Seventh member will strengthen any one of the three groups you send.
C. Team of Nine (2 + 2 + 2 + 2 + 1) :
Two students each from all the four groups mentioned above. The Ninth Member will strengthen any one of the four groups you send.

Event Structure

Qualification Round 1:

Round 1 is an Online Round which will be conducted in the month of September, 2020.Quizzes will be uploaded on the MindSpark website www.mind-spark.org, one for each of the mentioned groups.

The students of each group are supposed to solve the given quiz in the time allotted. The syllabus for the quiz will be uploaded on the same website in advance. The link to the quiz will remain active for 20 days. However, each team will be allowed only one attempt per quiz.

Qualification Round 2:

Round 2 will be conducted online during the Days of MindSpark’20. Four different events (one for each group) will be conducted. Details of the event will be disclosed on the day of the event.

However, any prerequisites will be mentioned along with Round 1 results on the website and also the links of round 2. Please note, team with 2 or 3 groups can participate in events of other branches.

Final Round 3:

Round 3 will be conducted online provided during the days of MindSpark’20. Further details of this round will be disclosed to the qualified teams on the day of the event.

Evaluation Scheme

Round 1 & 2

  1. Marks will be awarded out of 100.
  2. The highest scorer in each group will be awarded a 10 pointer
  3. Remaining participating teams in that particular group will be awarded a pointer with respect to the Score of the topper.
    Example: If the topper team for the civil group scores 68/100, the pointer for this team X will be 10. Another team, say Y with a score of 56, will be awarded a pointer with respect to the 68 of team X, i.e. (56/68)*10 = 8.2.
  4. A boundary pointer (e.g. 4.5) will be chosen. All scores above this boundary pointer will be considered Pass The ones below will be considered Fail. A Team will be considered qualified for the next round only when:
  5. A Team will be considered qualified for the next round only when:

    3 out of 4 groups qualify in a Team of 9.
    2 out of 3 groups qualify in a Team of 7.
    2 out of 2 groups qualify in a Team of 5.

Round 3

FAQ?
